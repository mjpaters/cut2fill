EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Two primary permit types exist for extracting sand, gravel and rock from
Queensland waterways under the Water Act 2000:

  1. QUARRY MATERIAL ALLOCATION NOTICES (QMAN) - 217 active records
     The primary commercial extraction permit. Authorises removal of sand,
     gravel, rock and other quarry materials from watercourses for sale/use.
     Includes allocated volumes, extraction tracking, and annual rate limits.

  2. RIVERINE PROTECTION PERMITS (RPP) - 18 active records
     Authorises excavation, fill placement and vegetation destruction in
     watercourses. Primarily for construction/infrastructure works, bank
     stabilisation, and crossing maintenance. Material removal for commercial
     purposes requires a separate QMAN.

Both datasets are publicly available via the QLD Open Data Portal, updated
quarterly, and accessible via CSV download and CKAN Datastore API.

RIVERINE PROTECTION PERMITS - DATA ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Total active RPPs: 18
All status: Granted

These are primarily construction/infrastructure permits, NOT commercial
extraction. Key distinction: RPP condition 8 typically states "Any material
removed from the watercourse must not be sold or used for beneficial purposes
unless authorised under the Water Act 2000 by a Quarry Material Allocation
Notice."

RPPs with sand/gravel extraction relevance:
  - RPP10000569/570 (Allan) - Boyne River, 12,500 m3 each - Sand extraction
    (but condition says material must NOT be removed from watercourse)
  - RPP10000599 (Bullerin) - Coomera River, 4,800 m3 - Sand, gravel, river-rock
  - RPP10000536 (Borgna/Bosnich) - Moongera Creek, 375 m3 - Sand, rock
  - RPP10000547 (QLD Hydro) - Yabba Creek, 540 m3 - Cobble, Gravel, Sand

Most RPPs are for bank stabilisation, crossing construction, flood damage
repair, and infrastructure projects - not commercial material supply.

RECOMMENDED APPROACH FOR CUT2FILL
================================================================================

1. PRIMARY DATA FEED: Use the CKAN Datastore API to pull QMAN data.
   The SQL endpoint allows sophisticated filtering:

   https://www.data.qld.gov.au/api/3/action/datastore_search_sql?sql=
   SELECT * FROM "2a7de874-b562-4712-9407-109332464f63"
   WHERE "Status" = 'Granted'
   AND "Remaining volume CubicMetres" > 0
   ORDER BY "Expiry date" DESC

2. REFRESH SCHEDULE: Data updates quarterly (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct).
   Set up automated pull on the 1st of each quarter month.

3. GEOCODING: Most QMAN records already have downstream/upstream lat/long
   coordinates. These map directly to the extraction reach on the waterway.

4. CROSS-REFERENCE: QMAN holders with remaining volume > 0 are active
   suppliers. Cross-reference with TMR quarry register data already in
   C:\cut2fill\docs\tmr-quarries-geocoded.json for overlap analysis.

5. KEY GAP: The "Material" field often says "Other" rather than specifying
   Sand/Gravel/Rock. The actual material is typically described in the
   Conditions text. A text parser could extract this.

6. VOLUME INTELLIGENCE: The extracted_volume vs allocated_volume ratio
   shows actual supply activity. Operators with high extraction rates
   are active commercial suppliers relevant to Cut2Fill.
