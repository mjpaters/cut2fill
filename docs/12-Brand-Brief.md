# Cut2Fill — Brand Identity Brief

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 7 April 2026
**Prepared by:** Archers Group Pty Ltd
**Purpose:** Define the Cut2Fill brand identity — logo, wordmark, tagline, colour, typography, and tone — to guide all design execution and communication.

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Cut2Fill** is a Queensland construction material sourcing platform — an interactive map and intelligence layer that connects earthworks suppliers, facilities, and projects across South East Queensland.

The platform serves everyone in the construction earthworks chain: site PMs, estimators, civil contractors, trucking companies, quarry operators, councils, and government agencies. It is free to use. It makes the complex simple.

**Domain:** cut2fill.com.au
**Shorthand:** C2F (expected to emerge as industry shorthand — design should accommodate this)

## 2. Brand Personality

### The Character

Cut2Fill is **your most useful mate on site — who also happens to be an engineer.**

- Talks like a contractor, thinks like an engineer
- Blunt, practical, no fluff — but never rude or exclusionary
- Doesn't make you feel stupid for not knowing technical detail
- When you need the technical depth, it's there and it's right
- Fast. The brand should feel like speed. "Where do I take this? Done."

### The Tone Spectrum

```
TOO CASUAL ←——————— CUT2FILL ———————→ TOO CORPORATE
Fillsites              ■              Procore/Aconex
"Free dirt mate"    HERE           "Enterprise solution"
```

Cut2Fill sits slightly left of centre — accessible first, professional always. A small contractor with one excavator feels just as welcome as BMD Group.

### Personality Traits

| Trait | What it means | What it's NOT |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| **Direct** | Says what it means. No marketing waffle. | Not aggressive or arrogant |
| **Accessible** | Every level of the industry feels welcome | Not dumbed down or patronising |
| **Quietly competent** | The technical depth is there but doesn't lead with jargon | Not showy about intelligence — "wow, that was easy" not "look how smart we are" |
| **Fast** | Everything communicates speed and efficiency | Not rushed or sloppy |
| **Grounded** | Literally about the ground. Earthy, real, physical. | Not abstract tech-startup energy |

### What Cut2Fill Never Says

- "AI-powered" or "data-driven" (the intelligence is invisible)
- "Revolutionising" or "disrupting" (construction people tune this out instantly)
- "Sustainable solution" as a headline (sustainability is the outcome, not the pitch)
- "Smart platform" or "intelligent matching" (users think "that was easy", not "that was smart")

### What Cut2Fill Does Say

- "See what's around you."
- "Know your options."
- "30 seconds, not 30 phone calls."
- "Your material, classified. Your options, mapped."

## 3. Logo Direction

### 3.1 The Concept: "The Exchange Mark"

Two forms in dynamic relationship — one representing **cut** (material leaving a site), one representing **fill** (material arriving at a site) — creating a single unified mark through their interaction.

The mark should suggest:
- **Movement** — material flowing from one place to another
- **Connection** — two parties finding each other, supply meeting demand
- **Professional scale** — this is infrastructure, not residential
- **Terrain** — could read as a topographic profile: a cut face meeting a fill profile

### 3.2 Visual Concept Options

**Concept A — "The Terrain Exchange"**
Two interlocking terrain profiles — a downward cut slope on the left flowing into an upward fill slope on the right. The negative space between them creates the exchange. Think of a cross-section diagram from a civil engineering drawing, abstracted into a mark.

*Why this works:* Every earthworks professional has seen a cut/fill diagram. It's instantly recognisable to the target audience without being literal. It's also abstract enough that non-construction people read it as "movement" and "connection."

**Concept B — "The Flow"**
Two abstract forms (could be curved, angular, or geometric) moving toward each other with a gap or channel between them. One descends (cut), one ascends (fill). The dynamic gap is the platform — the space where the exchange happens.

*Why this works:* More abstract, more versatile. Less tied to a specific visual metaphor. Works well animated (for web/social) with the two forms sliding into alignment.

**Concept C — "C2F Monogram"**
A custom-designed monogram incorporating C, 2, and F into a single geometric mark. The "2" serves as the bridge/connector between C and F. Could incorporate directional movement (arrows, flow lines) subtly within the letterforms.

*Why this works:* Strong for apparel, app icons, favicons, social profile images. The C2F shorthand becomes the logo itself. Risk: may feel too abstract for people who don't know the brand yet.

### 3.3 What the Logo Must NOT Be

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Recycle/circular arrows icon | Councils, waste companies, sustainability NGOs own this visual |
| Map pin | Google Maps, every location-based app. Generic. |
| Truck or excavator | Equipment hire territory (iSeekPlant, PlantMiner) |
| Leaf, tree, globe | Sustainability NGOs, not industry platforms |
| Generic "S" or swoosh | Says nothing, means nothing |
| Gradients or complexity | Must work in single colour at 16px on a phone screen |

### 3.4 Technical Requirements

The logo must work in ALL of these contexts:

| Context | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| **Phone screen** | Readable as favicon (16x16px) and app icon (48x48px) |
| **Website nav** | Icon + wordmark at ~24px height |
| **LinkedIn profile** | Square crop, recognisable at thumbnail size |
| **Government presentation** | Next to council logos, TMR, DES branding — must look equally polished |
| **Conference pull-up banner** | 2m tall, viewed from 5m away |
| **Hard hat sticker** | 50mm circle, single colour |
| **Email signature** | Small, clean, doesn't dominate |
| **Dark backgrounds** | Current site is dark theme — must work reversed |
| **Light backgrounds** | Government docs, printed reports, PowerPoint |
| **Single colour** | Fax (yes, really), photocopied docs, embossing |

### 3.5 Logo Lockups Required

1. **Icon only** — the mark, standalone (favicons, app icons, social avatars, hard hat stickers)
2. **Icon + wordmark horizontal** — primary lockup for navigation bars, email signatures, letterhead
3. **Icon + wordmark stacked** — for square/portrait formats, conference banners, social posts
4. **Wordmark only** — "Cut2Fill" in the brand typeface, for contexts where the icon is too small
5. **C2F abbreviated** — the shorthand mark for informal use, merch, community

## 4. Wordmark

### The Name Treatment

**Cut2Fill** — one word, capital C, numeral 2, capital F.

- The "2" is always a numeral, never "to" or "two"
- No space: "Cut2Fill" not "Cut 2 Fill"
- The "2" should feel like a natural bridge between Cut and Fill, not a break
- Consider whether the "2" gets a subtle design treatment (different weight, colour, or position) to emphasise the connection/exchange concept

### Typeface Direction

The wordmark typeface should be:
- **Geometric or semi-geometric sans-serif** — clean, modern, technical without being cold
- **Medium to bold weight** — confident, not delicate
- **Slightly condensed or normal width** — needs to read well in tight spaces (nav bars, phone screens)
- **No rounded/friendly typefaces** — this isn't a consumer app
- **No ultra-thin or light weights** — needs presence on dark and light backgrounds

Reference typefaces (for direction, not necessarily the final choice):
- **Inter** (current site font) — already works well, could be the wordmark face
- **DM Sans** — slightly more geometric, clean
- **Outfit** — modern geometric, good range of weights
- **General Sans** — has a grounded, industrial quality
- **Satoshi** — clean, slightly technical feel

## 5. Tagline

### Previous Tagline

**"Connect. Reuse. Sustain."** — Retired from primary use. Maps correctly to real platform values (connect = network, reuse = circular economy, sustain = impact) but reads as a sustainability values statement, not an action statement. Retained for formal sustainability-focused contexts only (government tenders, environmental reporting).

### Approved Tagline System

#### Primary Tagline (Brand)

**"Source. Verify. Build."**

Goes under the logo, on the banner, in the LinkedIn bio, on the business card. This is the brand identity.

| Word | Platform meaning | Audience reception |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Source** | Find your material — quarries, free fill, listings, options | PM: "I can find what I need." Quarry: "People will find me." |
| **Verify** | Compliance checking, fire ant zones, material classification, testing, chain of custody | Engineer: rigour. Government: credibility. PM: "I won't get fined." |
| **Build** | Get back to work. The whole point of the platform. | Everyone: "This gets out of my way and lets me do my job." |

"Verify" is the differentiator. No competitor globally has compliance/verification built in — they're all listing boards. This word separates Cut2Fill from Fillsites, Fill Wanted, and even Soil Connect.

#### Secondary Tagline (Campaign / Site Voice)

**"Find it. Verify it. Fill it."**

Used in marketing — LinkedIn posts, presentations to contractors, website hero sections. More conversational, more direct, plays on the brand name. Works alongside the primary tagline for different contexts.

This gives the brand two registers:
- **Boardroom voice:** Source. Verify. Build. (professional, universal, every stakeholder)
- **Site voice:** Find it. Verify it. Fill it. (direct, casual, action-oriented)

#### Functional Descriptor

**"QLD's construction material platform."**

Used where the tagline isn't enough context — Google search results, LinkedIn company description, government docs, first-contact contexts where people need to know WHAT it is.

#### Campaign Lines (Marketing Use)

| Line | Best used for |
|------|--------------|
| **"30 seconds, not 30 phone calls."** | LinkedIn posts, ad copy, presentations |
| **"The intelligence layer construction is missing."** | Website hero section, investor/government contexts |
| **"Know your options."** | Content series header, educational contexts |

## 6. Colour Palette

### Current Palette (Assessment)

The existing dark-theme palette is strong. It feels grounded, earthy, professional, and distinctly *not* generic tech-blue.

### Recommended Brand Colours

#### Primary

| Role | Colour | Hex | Usage |
|------|--------|-----|-------|
| **Brand Green** | Muted sage/olive | `#6b8f5e` | Primary action colour, logo, CTAs, active states |
| **Brand Amber** | Warm earth | `#c4843e` | Secondary accent, highlights, warnings, quarry markers |

#### Supporting

| Role | Colour | Hex | Usage |
|------|--------|-----|-------|
| **Slate Blue** | Muted teal | `#5c8a97` | Tertiary accent, links, info states |
| **Dark Ground** | Near-black brown | `#0f0d0a` | Primary background (landing page) |
| **Elevated Ground** | Dark brown | `#1a1814` | Card/panel backgrounds |
| **Light Text** | Warm white | `#e8e4dc` | Primary text on dark |
| **Muted Text** | Warm grey | `#9c9488` | Secondary text, labels |

#### Light Mode (Required for Government/Print)

| Role | Colour | Hex | Usage |
|------|--------|-----|-------|
| **Light Background** | Warm off-white | `#f5f3ef` | Government docs, print, light-mode future option |
| **Dark Text** | Near-black brown | `#1a1814` | Text on light backgrounds |
| **Brand Green on Light** | Same or slightly darkened | `#5a7a4f` | Ensure contrast ratio meets WCAG AA on light backgrounds |

### Colour Principles

1. **Green is the hero.** It's the brand. When you see that muted sage green in a construction context, it should mean Cut2Fill.
2. **Amber is the accent.** Warmth, earth, urgency. Used sparingly to draw attention.
3. **No bright/saturated colours.** The palette is deliberately muted — it reads as grounded and professional, not as a consumer app.
4. **Dark theme is primary** for digital. Light theme for print/government/formal docs.
5. **The green must own a lane.** Most construction tech is blue (Procore, Aconex, PlanGrid). Most sustainability is bright green. The muted sage/olive occupies unique space — industrial, natural, not eco-warrior.

## 7. Typography

### Digital (Web/App)

| Role | Font | Weight | Size Range |
|------|------|--------|------------|
| **Headings** | Inter | 700-800 | 17-45px |
| **Body** | Inter | 400 | 12-16px |
| **Labels/Tags** | Inter | 500-600, uppercase, tracked | 10-12px |
| **Data/Numbers** | Inter | 600-700 | Context-dependent |

Inter is already in use and working well. No reason to change for digital. It has excellent readability at small sizes (critical for phone use and map labels).

### Print / Formal Documents

Consider pairing Inter with a serif for formal government documents and reports:
- **Headings:** Inter Bold
- **Body:** A clean serif (e.g., Source Serif 4, Merriweather, or Libre Baskerville) adds gravitas in printed/PDF contexts without feeling old-fashioned

### Type Principles

1. **Uppercase tracked labels** for categories, section headers, badges — this is already done well in the current UI
2. **Tight letter-spacing on headings** (-0.5 to -2px) — creates the confident, compressed feel
3. **Generous line-height on body text** (1.5-1.7) — makes technical content digestible
4. **No more than two type sizes** visible at any one time on mobile — keep it clean

## 8. Voice & Messaging Guidelines

### How Cut2Fill Writes

| Principle | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Lead with the action, not the feature** | "Find fill within 50km" not "Our platform features geospatial search capabilities" |
| **Use dollar figures and specifics** | "$125/tonne to landfill" not "significant disposal costs" |
| **Speak in the user's language** | "Where do I send this material?" not "Material disposition optimisation" |
| **Be direct about what you don't do** | "We don't test material — we connect you with labs that do." |
| **Confidence without arrogance** | "604 verified facilities across SEQ" not "The most comprehensive database ever built" |

### Audience-Specific Tone Adjustments

| Audience | Adjustment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Site PM / Foreman** | Most direct. Shortest sentences. Action-first. "See what's near your site." |
| **Estimator** | Numbers-led. Cost comparisons. "Compare quarry vs reuse vs landfill." |
| **Company Director** | Bottom-line focus. "Your last project paid $1.2M in levy. This one doesn't have to." |
| **Quarry Operator** | Their perspective. "Every PM in SEQ looking for material sees your quarry." |
| **Council / Government** | Slightly more formal. Policy alignment. "Real-time material flow visibility for your LGA." |
| **Media** | The big number hook. "571,000 tonnes of reusable material goes to landfill every year." |

## 10. Execution — Tools & Services

### Logo Design

| Option | Cost | Turnaround | Quality | Best For |
|--------|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Fiverr / 99designs** | $50-500 | 3-7 days | Variable, often mediocre | NOT recommended for primary brand mark |
| **Australian freelance designer** | $1,500-5,000 | 2-4 weeks | High | Best balance of cost, quality, and collaboration |
| **Design agency (small/boutique)** | $5,000-15,000 | 4-8 weeks | Very high | If budget allows — you get brand system, not just a logo |
| **AI-assisted design (Midjourney/DALL-E) + designer refinement** | $500-2,000 | 1-2 weeks | Good — AI generates concepts, designer refines into production-ready vectors | Fastest path to a strong result. Recommended. |

**Recommended approach:** Use AI image tools (Midjourney, DALL-E, Ideogram) to generate 20-30 concept explorations based on this brief. Shortlist 3-5 directions. Then engage a freelance designer ($500-1,500) to take the best concept and produce production-ready vector files, all lockups, colour variations, and a basic brand guidelines PDF.

### AI Logo Exploration Tools

| Tool | Strength | How to Use |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Midjourney** | Best for abstract/artistic concepts, strong at geometric marks | Prompt with brief concepts, iterate on style |
| **Ideogram** | Best for text/wordmark integration, handles "Cut2Fill" text well | Use for wordmark + icon combinations |
| **DALL-E (ChatGPT)** | Good for quick iteration, easy to describe modifications | Fast concept exploration |
| **Recraft** | Purpose-built for logo/icon design, vector output | Best for production-ready icon exploration |
| **Looka / Brandmark** | AI logo generators — template-based but fast | Good for seeing many variations quickly, weak on originality |

### Designer Sourcing

| Platform | How to Find |
|----------|------------|
| **Dribbble** | Search "construction logo" or "industrial brand identity" — contact designers whose style matches |
| **Behance** | Same approach — filter by Australian designers for timezone alignment |
| **99designs (1-to-1)** | Skip the contest model, hire a single designer directly based on portfolio |
| **Local (Brisbane)** | Search "brand identity designer Brisbane" — meeting in person for a construction brand has value |

### Brand Guidelines Document

Once the logo is finalised, the designer should deliver:
- Logo files: SVG, PNG (transparent), PDF, EPS
- All 5 lockups (icon, horizontal, stacked, wordmark, C2F)
- Colour specifications: HEX, RGB, CMYK, Pantone
- Clear space rules and minimum size
- Do/don't usage examples
- Typography specification
- 2-4 page PDF brand guidelines document
